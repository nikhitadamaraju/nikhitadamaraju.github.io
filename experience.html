Research Projects

Dual Degree Thesis, Indian Institute of Technology Madras, India

Robert Bosch Center for Data Science and AI
July, 2019 – May, 2020
Thesis advisor: Dr. Himanshu Sinha

  • Assessed multiple gestational age (GA) estimation models on preterm birth prediction for a cohort of North-Indian women.
  • Implemented feature selection and designed a regression-based model for first trimester GA estimation that has the highest preterm birth labelling accuracy [1].
  • Designed a random forest-based model using ultrasound metrics to predict GA accurately in second and third trimesters.
  • Worked in close collaboration with a team of clinicians at the Translational Health Sciences Institute, Delhi as a part of Bill and Melinda Gates foundation grant, Grand Challenges of India grant and BIRAC grant.
  • Presented research at multiple Data Science and AI academic conferences.

Stanford University CA

Summer Intern, Department of Biochemistry
May – August, 2019
Advisor: Dr. Julia Salzman

  • Designed a pipeline from scratch that implements generalized linear modelling on transcriptomic data to identify conserved features of genomic repeats that facilitate alternative splicing in primates.
  • Correlated genomic and transcriptomic data to identify genomic repeat-based features conserved across primate families.
  • Identified several conserved SINE and LINE repeat-based features that facilitate the formation of alternately spliced transcripts.
  • Analyzed the statistical significance of the presence of conserved genomic signatures across the genome.

Khorana scholar, Stanford University CA

May – August, 2018
Advisor: Dr. Julia Salzman

  • Selected as one of the 40 Khorana fellowship grant recipients among 1000 applicants, 2018 to conduct research at Stanford School of Medicine.
  • Designed a bioinformatic pipeline for downstream analysis of Epstein Barr virus infected human B-cell transcriptomic datasets downloaded from SRA for lytic and latent infection stages of the virus.
  • Characterized the presence of circRNAs and alternative splicing present in LMP-2A and LMP-2B genes in Epstein-Barr virus validating previous experimental results.
  • Summarized research in the form a detailed report to be presented at the department research seminar.
